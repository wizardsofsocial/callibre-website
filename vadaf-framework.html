VADAF: a 5-part QA framework for Voice & Chat AI

VADAF is the evaluation framework Callibre uses to score every interaction your voice and chat agents have with customers. It breaks quality down into five dimensions — Validation, Accuracy, Decisioning, Action, and Flow — so you can see exactly why an interaction succeeded or failed.

Built for HighLevel voice AI agents

Why VADAF exists

Traditional QA or spot-checking tells you that a call was “good” or “bad”, but not why. VADAF separates failures into clear, actionable buckets so operators, agencies, and product teams know what to fix:

  • Validation problems: the agent never confirmed the right inputs.
  • Accuracy problems: the content was wrong or hallucinated.
  • Decisioning problems: it chose the wrong path or escalation.
  • Action problems: it failed to trigger the right downstream actions.
  • Flow problems: the conversation felt confusing, robotic, or non-compliant.

In Callibre, every test run and production call can be scored against VADAF so your dashboards and reports show exactly where agents are breaking – not just that they are.

The 5 VADAF dimensions

V — Validation

Did the agent validate the right inputs?

Validation checks whether the agent confirmed required information before taking action.

  • Confirmed caller intent before transferring.
  • Verified location before giving location-specific info.
  • Collected required fields like name, phone, or account ID.
  • Clarified ambiguity before starting critical workflows.

Failure examples: booking an appointment without confirming date/time, giving pricing without validating the product or location.

A — Accuracy

Was the information factually correct?

Accuracy measures whether the agent’s responses match the source of truth — your knowledge base, policies, and systems.

  • Correct hours, pricing, and services.
  • Correct KB article or troubleshooting steps.
  • No hallucinated policies, numbers, or claims.

Failure examples: incorrect hours, wrong instructions, or made-up policies and pricing.

D — Decisioning

Did the agent choose the right path?

Decisioning evaluates how well the agent classifies intent and chooses the correct flow given context.

  • Routing Sales vs Support correctly.
  • Escalating when escalation is required.
  • Choosing between self-service content vs human handoff.

Failure examples: sending a support caller to Sales, escalating when not needed, or failing to escalate when required.

A — Action

Did the agent trigger the right actions?

Action measures whether downstream system actions were fired correctly and at the right time.

  • Triggering transfers when required.
  • Sending SMS follow-ups or KB links.
  • Booking appointments with the right details.
  • Firing the right internal workflows.

Failure examples: missing a required transfer, triggering the wrong flow, or firing actions too early, too late, or multiple times.

F — Flow

Did the conversation feel clear, natural, and compliant?

Flow looks at conversational structure and experience.

  • Asking one question at a time when required.
  • Following prompt rules and guardrails.
  • Avoiding repetition, looping, or contradictions.

Failure examples: asking multiple questions at once, robotic or confusing phrasing, repeated questions, or breaking required prompt rules.
